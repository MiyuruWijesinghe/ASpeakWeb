ASpeak

A Mobile Application Approach to Translate Sri Lankan Sign Language to Text in Real-Time

Get Started

What is ASpeak?

ASpeak is an application that translates static and dynamic sign language gestures into text in real-time basis. It is a cost-effective and user-friendly solution as it has been developed in the form of a mobile application. This application has below components.

Let’s Talk

This component can be used to communicate between normal people and aurally handicapped people as an interpreter.

Word To Sign

This component allows user to type a particular word and see the relevant sign generated on the screen.

Learning Corner

When the user is navigated to this screen, the mobile camera turns on and loads to the camera frame. When user presses "Start Detection" button, real-time object detection starts and the relevant output displays to the user.

Upload & Convert

This component allows user to upload either a dynamic sign or static sign. Then generate the prediction and display it to the user in the output section when the "Generate Prediction" button is pressed.

Background

Sign language is a form of visual language that uses hand gestures and body language to convey meaning. Aristotle was the first to have a claim recorded about the deaf. His theory was that people can only learn through hearing spoken language. Deaf people were therefore seen as being unable to learn or be educated at all. Geronimo Cardano, an Italian mathematician, and physician was probably the first scholar to identify that learning does not require hearing. He discovered, in the 1500s, that the deaf were able to be educated by using written words. after that sign languages have developed and evolved over time as a convenient means of communication.

In Today most of the time deaf and mute people mainly use sign language to communicate among fellow deaf-mute people. The reason is being that most general people don’t know sign language. When deaf-mute people communicate with normal people, they use methods such as writing on paper, using body language or texting. A method such as writing on paper or texting always highlights their inability to properly communicate and thus makes them somewhat distant from society. And of course, by using just body language they cannot convey the full message that they want to convey. Nowadays because of the technological advances, there are few products translating text to sign language or translating voice to sign language. However, most of the products fail bring satisfactory results or user friendly for a deaf-mute person’s daily life. Further details regarding existing products will be discussed in under further topics. Because of such reasons, it is safe to say that currently there is no proper method or technology to make deaf-mute people communicate with normal people without feeling any discomfort.

Research Gap

The main thing is there is no real-time dynamic sign word identifying a product for general usage because most Sri Lankan researchers had been focused on the static sign and static-dynamic letters only.

Most researches are not implemented to identify signs in real-time. They take recorded video as input. That applications implemented to dynamic and static sign identification but it can be able to identify only 8 dynamic signs. In general, we cannot communicate using 8 dynamic signs because most signs are dynamic signs. Also there are some applications which identify only on Sinhala sign letters. Using letters, we cannot communicate in real-time because it takes a lot of time to say something. If someone needs to say one word, he wants to show all the letters one by one in the word. It is very difficult.

Most applications cannot identify dynamic sign words. So we cannot generally communicate without dynamic signs because most signs are dynamic. It means if someone shows "I went" using dynamic sign we predict the next dynamic word without input sing. In this example, we can predict the next word as home, school, or some other place.

Research Problems

  • Communication barrier between Sri Lankan Hearing/Aurally handicapped community and the public.
  • Unavailability of a product capable of facilitating effective communication.
  • Usable and inexpensive product.
  • How to identify key moments of a sign and add pre-processing to a real-time video feed ? .
  • How to detect whether the sign is a static or a dynamic.
  • Most Applications can not identify dynamic signs.
  • Can identify very fewer amount dynamic signs.

Research Objectives

Main objective of this project is to implement an easier way of communication between deaf, mute people and the people who do not understand sign languages for the purpose of providing the people with hearing impairments and speaking disabilities a better way of reaching out to the outer world. “ASpeak” is our mobile app which implemented achieved our primary object. It takes real-time signs and converts them into Sinhala text and it contains a learning part also. If some interested to learn sign language he or she can learn through our app. The main objectives of this application are listed below.

  • Pre-process & Determine Sign Type.
  • Static Gesture Classifier.
  • Dynamic Gesture Classifier.
  • Generate Sinhala Text .
